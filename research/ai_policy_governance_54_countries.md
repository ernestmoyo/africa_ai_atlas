# Africa AI Atlas -- AI Policy & Governance Data (All 54 Countries)

**Compiled:** March 2026
**Prepared by:** 7Square Inc.

## Methodology & Key Sources

Data compiled from multiple authoritative sources including:
- UNESCO Global AI Ethics and Governance Observatory
- UNCTAD Data Protection and Privacy Legislation Worldwide tracker
- Data Protection Africa (ALT Advisory) -- dataprotection.africa
- AI Policy Lab Africa -- aipolicy.africa
- Intelpoint (July 2025 report on 16 countries with AI strategies)
- Digital Policy Alert -- Data Protection in Africa Roundup 2025
- African Union Continental AI Strategy documentation
- OECD AI Policy Observatory
- Individual country government publications and news sources

## Legend

- **AI Strategy:** Yes (published) / In development / No
- **Data Protection Law:** Yes (enacted) / Draft / None
- **AI Ethics Framework:** Yes (formal) / Partial (within strategy or charter) / No
- **Gov AI Body:** Yes (dedicated) / Advisory / None
- **Open Data Portal:** Yes (comprehensive government portal) / Limited (via AfDB/Open Data for Africa only) / None

## Important Notes

1. **Data currency:** This data reflects the best available information as of March 2026. The AI policy landscape in Africa is rapidly evolving, with multiple countries actively drafting or revising their frameworks.

2. **African Union Continental AI Strategy:** Endorsed July 2024 at the 45th AU Executive Council session in Accra. Phase I (2025-2026) focuses on governance framework creation, national AI strategies, and capacity building.

3. **Africa Declaration on AI:** Signed April 4, 2025 in Kigali by 54 African states, jointly endorsed by the AU, Smart Africa, and member governments. This is the most unified AI policy framework on the continent.

4. **Open Data for Africa (AfDB):** The African Development Bank's Africa Information Highway provides open data platforms for virtually all 54 countries, though the depth of content varies significantly. Countries marked "Limited" have only this continental platform without a comprehensive national government open data portal.

5. **Zambia's strategy** (2024-2026) is noteworthy for being among the earliest comprehensive AI strategies from a lower-income African country.

6. **Data Protection trend:** By early 2026, approximately 44-46 African countries have data protection laws, up from 36 in January 2024. The Gambia (Sep 2025) and Djibouti (Jun 2025) were among the most recent to enact laws.
