# Africa AI Atlas -- Talent & Education Data (All 54 Countries)

**Compiled: March 2026 | 7Square Inc.**

## Data Sources & Methodology

- **Tertiary Enrollment %**: World Bank / UNESCO UIS via IndexMundi (most recent available year, varying 2018-2023 depending on country)
- **STEM Graduates %**: UNESCO UIS, WEF, OECD, CSET Georgetown (share of tertiary graduates in STEM fields)
- **AI/ML Programs**: EduRank university AI rankings, AIMS network, university websites, TechCabal
- **Developer Activity**: GitHub Innovation Graph (data through Q4 2024, published early 2025), GitHub Octoverse 2024, CoinLaw/SQ Magazine GitHub Statistics 2026, African Exponent, earlier surveys (2021) for smaller markets
- **AI Publications**: Scopus bibliometric analysis of ML research in Africa 1993-2021 (PMC/Springer study, n=2,761 documents)
- **Digital Literacy / Internet Penetration**: ITU Facts & Figures 2024, Statista, AI Talent Readiness Index for Africa 2025

## Key Qualitative Insights

**Countries with NO identified AI/ML publications**: Angola, Cabo Verde, Central African Republic, Comoros, Djibouti, Equatorial Guinea, Eritrea, Sao Tome and Principe, Seychelles, South Sudan (10 countries)

**Notable AI/ML Programs & Institutions**:
- **AIMS Network**: Centres in South Africa, Senegal, Ghana, Cameroon, Rwanda (with AMMI ML masters program)
- **Carnegie Mellon University Africa**: Kigali, Rwanda (5 students + 2 instructors presented at NeurIPS 2025)
- **OpenAI Academy**: University of Lagos, Nigeria (first in Africa, October 2025)
- **Google AI Lab**: Accra, Ghana (also home to AMMI founder/director)
- **AIMS AI for Science Masters**: Partnership with DeepMind, South Africa (Sept 2026 cohort applications open)
- **UM6P**: Mohammed VI Polytechnic University, Morocco (AI research hub)
- **UCT CAIR**: Centre for Artificial Intelligence Research (Ethics of AI, AI for Development)
- **U. Pretoria AfriDSAI**: African Institute of Data Science and AI ("Abstracts into Indigenous Voices" project)
- **Stellenbosch U.**: MSc ML&AI + Data and AI Ethics for Africa Public Square
- **Wits University**: MSc in AI/ML/Robotics (computational intelligence, computer vision, multi-agent systems)
- **U. Johannesburg**: Master of Artificial Intelligence (cross-disciplinary)
- **MANCOSA**: Postgraduate Diploma in Artificial Intelligence (South Africa)
- **Ain Shams University**: AI smart assistant project with Microsoft/Egypt Ministry of Higher Education (2025)

**Major AI Training Initiatives (2025-2026)**:
- **Microsoft Elevate**: Target of training 3 million Africans in AI skills in 2026; partnership with MTN (~300M subscribers); ZAR 5.4B ($330M) cloud/AI infrastructure investment in SA (completion 2026); 1M South Africans targeted by 2026; 350K+ Nigerians trained (2025); Education Plus AI/digital skills platform launched (March 2026)
- **Google**: AI Skilling Blueprint for Africa ($7.5M commitment + $2.25M for data infrastructure); free Gemini AI Pro for students in 8 African countries (Egypt, Ghana, Kenya, Morocco, Nigeria, Rwanda, SA, Zimbabwe); Hustle Academy AI training for SMEs; Google for Startups Accelerator: AI First (Africa)
- **AWS**: re/Start program expanding across Africa (12 cohorts since Oct 2020; Kenya, South Africa, Ghana); Skills Center in Cape Town; 18-week cloud intensive in Accra, Ghana
- **Huawei**: "AI 100 Schools, 100,000 Talents" initiative; ICT Academy partnerships
- **GitHub/Microsoft**: GenAI for Youth Program for African developers

**Regional Digital Divides**:
- Internet penetration ranges from ~91% (Morocco) to ~10% (South Sudan)
- Urban-rural internet gap: 57% vs 23% (ITU 2024)
- Gender gap: 43% men vs 31% women online in Africa (ITU 2024)
- Mobile internet costs in Africa are 14x higher than in Europe
